# Currency: Kin and Kithe

## The Foundation

The monetary system is built on one principle: a human being has value simply by existing. Not by working, not by producing, not by contributing measurably to any output — just by being present in a community of people who are committed to each other.

This is the anchor. Everything else follows from it.

---

## The Kin

The **kin** is the local currency of every community. It is issued by the community's Central Bank and exists only within that community. It cannot leave.

**1 kin = 1/10,000 of a person-year of presence.**

Because the unit is defined by presence rather than labor output, a child, an elder, someone recovering from illness, and a highly skilled specialist all generate the same person-year at the same rate. This is intentional. The currency is humanist by construction, not by policy.

### How kin is created

The Central Bank has two legitimate mechanisms for creating kin:

1. **Presence income** — every member receives a continuous kin issuance simply for being a community member. This is unconditional. It is the community saying: *you are here, and that has value.*

2. **Conversion** — when a member earns kithe externally (through federation-level work or inter-community trade) and wishes to spend it locally, the Central Bank retires the kithe and mints the equivalent kin. New kin is genuinely created in this operation. The community is importing external value and denominating it locally.

### How kin is destroyed

The Central Bank has two legitimate mechanisms for destroying kin:

1. **Demurrage** — large kin balances above a community-defined threshold decay slowly over time. The destroyed kin flows into the community commons. This keeps the currency circulating and prevents structural wealth concentration within the community.

2. **External purchase** — when the community collectively decides to acquire something from outside (federation-priced goods, institutional services, external resources), kin is drawn from the community commons pool and destroyed. This generates the kithe needed for the external transaction.

### What kin cannot do

Kin has no existence outside the community that issued it. It cannot be transferred to a member of another community, cannot be converted to kithe by an individual, and cannot be used to purchase kithe-priced goods. These are not technical limitations — they are the structural guarantee that the local economy cannot be drained by external actors.

## The Kithe

The **kithe** is the federation-layer unit of account. Unlike kin, individuals can hold kithe — but it is a different instrument used for a different purpose.

**1 kithe = 1 kin in denomination.** They are not interchangeable by design; they simply share the same scale because both are grounded in person-years.

### How individuals earn kithe

**Presence income (5,000 kithe/year):** Every member of a federated community receives a kithe issuance from the federation on their birthday, simply for being present in the network. This mirrors the kin presence floor at the local level — the federation is saying: *you exist in this wider network, and that has value.* It is unconditional.

**Earned kithe:**
- Working for an institutional community (a federation hospital, a chip fabrication facility, a research institution)
- Selling goods or services priced in kithe to members of other communities
- Performing federation-level work contracted by the Congress

### What kithe can purchase

Things that communities choose to price in kithe — typically goods and services that are explicitly offered for inter-community trade, and goods produced by federation-commons institutions. Communities decide what they price in each currency. Goods priced in kin are not accessible to kithe.

### The one-way valve

Members may convert kithe to kin at the community Central Bank. The Central Bank retires the kithe and creates the equivalent kin. The community's kin supply increases; the member can now spend locally.

The reverse does not exist. Kin cannot be converted to kithe by any individual. Kin can only leave the local economy through collective community decision — a governance act, not a market act.

This asymmetry is the anti-extraction guarantee. An outside actor holding kithe cannot buy their way into the local economy. A wealthy member cannot drain the community's purchasing power outward. The only outflow is through the commons, decided democratically.

## Community-Level Kithe

Communities, not just individuals, have kithe positions. A community's aggregate kithe balance reflects its net trade position with the rest of the federation — the sum of its members' kithe activity plus federation presence grants.

### Community presence grants

Just as a community issues kin to members for being present, the federation issues kithe to communities for being present in the network. The rate is proportional to the community's active membership — a community of 500 members accumulates more kithe than a community of 50, because more presence is being contributed to the federation as a whole.

The community presence grant is issued periodically by the federation to the community's commonwealth kithe account. It requires:
- The community node is active and reachable
- The community has a minimum number of active members (preventing shell communities)
- The community is in good standing with its clearing obligations

This means a community can fund inter-community trade and contribute to federation-scale projects without depending on individual members to donate or spend their personal kithe. The commonwealth kithe account is the community's institutional presence in the wider economy.

The grant rate is a federation governance parameter, set by the Congress and adjustable over time. The presence symmetry is the principle; the specific rate is a policy decision.

When the community makes an external purchase using kithe:
- Kin is destroyed from the community commons pool
- Kithe is spent to the external counterparty

When the community receives kithe (from a favorable trade balance or federation redistribution):
- Members may convert it inward via the Central Bank, creating kin
- Or it sits as a community kithe reserve for future external purchases

The community's kithe balance is a reporting tool — it makes the community's external trade position visible and subject to democratic scrutiny. A persistent kithe deficit is a signal, not a crisis. It means the community is consuming more from the federation than it is contributing, and that is a community conversation to have.

## The Federal Commons and Institutional Communities

Some productive infrastructure is too large, too specialized, or too strategically important to belong to any single community. A chip fabrication facility, a pharmaceutical production center, a major research institution — these are federation commons, collectively owned and governed by the Congress of Federations.

Federation-commons institutions operate as a special class of community:
- They hold kin accounts and pay their workers in kin
- They are funded by the Congress through kithe allocation, not by member presence income
- Their goods and services are priced in kithe, accessible to any community in the federation
- Their workers may be members of the institution itself as a community, or seconded from geographic communities

This means no single geographic community can own or extract rent from federation-scale infrastructure. Access is governed, not purchased.

---

## Demurrage at the Federation Level

The Congress may apply demurrage to community kithe surpluses above a federation-defined threshold. Excess kithe is levied into the federal commons pool, which the Congress then allocates to:

- Communities with persistent kithe deficits
- Institutional communities requiring operating funds
- Federation-scale infrastructure investment

This is the Bancor mechanism: surplus communities are incentivized to spend their kin back into the federation rather than accumulate structural creditor power over deficit communities. A community that grows food abundantly and exports it widely gets paid fully at transaction time. It does not also get to hold permanent leverage over the rest of the federation in perpetuity.

The levy applies only above a threshold. Moderate community reserves are healthy and encouraged. Chronic, compounding surpluses that function as structural claims on other communities' futures are what get rebalanced.

## The Full Monetary Circuit

| Operation | Kin | Kithe | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| Member presence income | created | — | Community Central Bank → member |
| Member birthday issuance | — | created | Federation → member |
| Community presence grant | — | created | Federation → community commonwealth |
| Member converts kithe inward | created | retired | Community Central Bank |
| Community external purchase | destroyed | spent | Community commons |
| Individual demurrage | destroyed | — | Community Central Bank → commons |
| Federation demurrage levy | — | transferred | Community → federal commons |
| Federation commons funding | — | allocated | Federal commons → institution |

The kin supply at any moment is: presence income issued, plus net inward conversions, minus external purchases made, minus demurrage collected. It is always auditable and always traceable to real decisions made by real people.

---

## The Presence Floor

The community's kin supply always has a floor: the sum of all members' accumulated presence income. This floor cannot be breached by any governance decision, because presence income flows directly into individual member accounts — not through the commons, not through any pool that governance controls.

Even in the worst case — a fully captured governance that authorizes every possible external purchase and drains the community commons entirely — members still hold what the Central Bank has issued to them directly. The commons can be emptied. Individual presence income cannot be touched.

This means the minimum kin in circulation at any moment is always at least equal to the total human presence the community contains. The currency is, at its floor, a direct expression of the people who are there.

---

## Why This Prevents Extraction

Wealth extraction requires a mechanism by which value can be moved from where it was created to somewhere else, without the consent of the people who created it. This system closes every such mechanism:

- **Kin cannot leave the community** — there is nothing for an extractor to take
- **Kithe-priced goods are not local goods** — external purchasing power cannot touch kin-priced housing, food, or services
- **Conversion is inward-only** — external wealth can enter the local economy but not drain it
- **External purchases require collective decision** — the outflow valve is held by democratic governance, not by market forces or individual actors
- **Demurrage prevents accumulation** — neither individuals nor communities can build permanent structural power through hoarding
- **The presence floor is unconditional** — even governance capture cannot extract what has already been issued to members as presence income

The only way to extract from a community using this system is to capture its governance *and* wait for future presence income to accumulate in the commons before members receive it. That window is narrow, visible, and subject to member revolt. It is a political problem, not a monetary one — and it is the right problem to solve politically.
