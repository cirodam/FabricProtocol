# Universal Rights

These are rights that hold in every community within the federation, regardless of local rules, culture, or member votes. No community may override them. They are the floor below which no local autonomy extends.

They are deliberately narrow. The purpose is not to encode a complete political philosophy but to identify the near-universal consensus across nearly all coherent ethical and cultural frameworks. Contested political positions — even widely held ones — belong to local governance, not here.

---

## Physical Integrity

**No murder.** A community may not execute a member, and no member may kill another without facing federation-level accountability. This includes killing by official community actors.

**No torture.** No cruel, degrading, or deliberately painful treatment as punishment or coercion, regardless of what a member is accused of.

**No slavery.** No person may be compelled to labor under threat of violence or imprisonment. Labor obligations must be voluntary and agreed to.

**No sexual coercion.** No rape, no coerced sexual contact, no using position or resource control to extract sexual compliance.

---

## Procedural Rights

**No arbitrary imprisonment.** A person may not be detained indefinitely without a specific charge and a hearing. Confinement pending a hearing must be brief and proportional.

**Right to a hearing.** Before any significant sanction is imposed — expulsion, long-term confinement, large resource forfeiture — the accused must have an opportunity to respond to the charge and present their account.

**Proportional punishment.** Sanctions must bear a reasonable relationship to the offense. Permanent expulsion for a minor infraction, or collective punishment of family members for an individual's actions, are not permitted.

---

## Exit Rights

**You may leave.** No community may physically prevent a member from departing. Locking someone in — through confinement, debt bondage, or controlling their only access to food or shelter — is not permitted.

**Children may not be held hostage.** A member's minor children cannot be retained by a community to coerce the parent's continued membership or compliance.

**Portable resources on exit.** A departing member is entitled to their accrued balance and portable identity record. A community may not confiscate these as a condition of exit or as punishment for leaving. (The specific transfer mechanics are defined in the federation's economic charter.)

---

## Inherent Economic Standing

Every person holds economic standing by virtue of their existence — not from the moment they join a community, but from the moment they were born. A person's life before joining a community is not economically invisible. They arrive as someone who has already accumulated years of existence: labor potential developed, knowledge acquired, social relationships formed, capacity built. That prior life has value, and a community that admits them is receiving all of it.

This right has three concrete implications:

**The floor cannot be conditioned on performance.** A member's access to the community's economic floor — housing, food, healthcare — may not be made contingent on labor output, social standing, or approval by other members. The floor exists because the person is a member. It does not have to be earned.

**Joining credit is proportional to age.** When a person joins a community, the kin issued to them reflects their full age, not just the fact of their arrival. A person who has lived forty years brings forty years of accumulated existence into the community. The monetary system expresses this by crediting them accordingly. A community may not issue a new adult member only a single year's worth of standing — that would pretend their prior life did not happen.

**Standing is immediate on admission.** No waiting period, probationary status, or initial exclusion from the monetary system may delay a new member's economic standing. Communities may set reasonable admission criteria, but once admitted, the member's full standing — including the credit for their prior years — is recognized immediately.

This right connects directly to the monetary system. The person-year is the fundamental unit not because it measures output but because existence over time is itself the real economic contribution being recognized. The issuance of kin is not a policy gift. It is the community's acknowledgment of what the person already is.

---

## Community Economic Sovereignty

Every community within the federation holds a floor of economic capacity that no external actor may extract below. This applies to other communities, to the federation itself, to creditors, to trade partners, and to any other entity with economic relationships to the community.

**The floor cannot be levied away.** No federation assessment, inter-community debt obligation, or trade agreement may reduce a community's capacity below what is required to maintain its members' economic floor — housing, food, healthcare. External obligations are real and must be honored, but they are subordinate to the community's internal floor. A creditor community cannot enforce a debt that would cause another community's members to go hungry or lose shelter.

**Resources essential to the floor are not alienable.** A community may not sell, pledge, or transfer the land, equipment, or infrastructure that is necessary to maintain its members' basic provision — not to outside actors, and not even by internal referendum. These assets are held in trust for current and future members. They exist outside ordinary economic exchange.

**The federation cannot extract below the floor.** Any levy, fee, or assessment imposed at the federation level must be means-tested against each community's capacity. Communities at or near their floor threshold are exempt until capacity recovers. The federation's claim on community resources is always residual — it comes after the community's own obligations to its members.

This right is distinct from political autonomy. It does not prevent the federation from setting rules, adjudicating disputes, or imposing consequences for rights violations. It prevents the federation — or anyone else — from using economic extraction as a mechanism of control or punishment in ways that harm ordinary members.

---

## What This List Is Not

This list does not address:

- **Free expression** — every community has sacred limits; they simply disagree on which ones. This is local.
- **Property rights** — scope and extent are deeply contested. Local.
- **Drug and alcohol use, dress, diet** — wildly variable across cultures. Local.
- **Reproductive decisions** — genuinely contested. Communities may have norms; the federation does not.
- **Who holds full membership rights** — communities define membership criteria within their covenants.

The UN Declaration of Human Rights is a useful reference but it mixes genuine universals with contested liberal political positions. This document does not.

---

## Enforcement

Violations of these rights are handled at the federation level, not the local level. A member who believes a community has violated one of these rights may bring a complaint to the inter-community arbitration process. If a pattern of violation is established, the federation may suspend or expel the community from the network.

Individual communities do not adjudicate their own compliance with these rights — that would be self-serving. The arbitration panel is selected by sortition from across the broader federation.
